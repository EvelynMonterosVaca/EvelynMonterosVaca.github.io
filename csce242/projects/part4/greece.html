Greece

Information

Greece stands proudly as the southernmost country of the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by its impressive mountainous terrain that has historically shaped its internal communications. Its extensive coastline has been a gateway for trade throughout the ages. With a land area comparable to England or Alabama, Greece is also home to around 2,000 islands, of which approximately 170 are inhabited.

Athens, the vibrant capital, has experienced significant growth since the late 20th century and is a vital part of the Attikí region, which accommodates nearly one-third of the nation's population. Strategically located at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa, Greece features a highly indented coastline that ranks as the 11th longest in the world at an impressive 13,676 kilometers (8,498 miles). The country's land boundary measures 1,160 kilometers (721 miles), and it lies between latitudes 34° and 42° N and longitudes 19° and 30° E. Its extreme points are marked by Ormenio in the North and Gavdos Island, the southernmost point of Europe, in the South.
